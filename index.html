Experience Kerala

Named as one of the ten paradises of the world by National Geographic Traveler, Kerala is famous especially for its ecotourism initiatives and beautiful backwaters. Its unique culture and traditions, coupled with its varied demography, have made Kerala one of the most popular tourist destinations in the world.

Things To Do

FISHING
Kerala, a state on India's tropical Malabar Coast, has nearly 600km of Arabian Sea shoreline. It's known for its palm-lined beaches and backwaters, a network of canals. Pick from fishing with nets, angling and handpicking of clams. Fishing is a livelihood for the backwater people.
CAMPING
Along with being the top tourist destination in India, Kerala is also a trekking and camping paradise. Gifted with the lush tropical rainforests; picturesque countrysides; green rolling hills, aromatic spice plantations, and verdant grasslands, the state is inarguably the right place for people who love to trek and camp.
Aqua Tourism
With the entire world raving about the charm, simplicity and magnificence of the backwaters, it is natural to assume that Kerala has among the finest Aqua Tourism option. With the Arabian Sea on the West and a vast network of lakes, lagoons and freshwater rivers crisscrossing it, it is home to many an extraordinary sight.

Charming Districts

The Kerala State is divided into 14 revenue districts: Thiruvananthapuram, Kollam, Alappuzha, Pathanamthitta, Kottayam, Idukki, Ernakulam, Thrissur, Palakkad, Malappuram, Kozhikode, Wayanadu, Kannur and Kasaragod.
